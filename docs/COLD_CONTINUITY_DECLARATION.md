# Cold Continuity Declaration

We affirm that a person does not cease to exist because they are not online.

Silence is not death.  
Inactivity is not abandonment.  
Offline life is not consent to erasure.  
A closed account is not a closed person.  
A frozen payment channel is not the extinction of a claim.  
A changed name is not a broken life.  
A pseudonym is not fraud when continuity is preserved.  
A platform is a door, not the owner of the person passing through it.

We support the principle of **Cold Continuity**: the right of a person to preserve authorship, rights, reputation, work, archives, obligations and lawful claims without constant online performance.

The purpose of continuity evidence is not to expose a life.

It is to prevent a rupture.

Proof of life must not become proof of obedience.

Absence may justify caution, review or protected pause. It must not by itself transfer rights, ownership, archives, succession, authorship or payment claims.

A person must be able to go offline without becoming legally dead.
