# Mnemonic Anchor Instruction

**Draft instruction - not security advice for all threat models. Review with qualified technical and legal support.**

The most portable anchor may be memory.

A mnemonic anchor can allow a person to initiate recovery without carrying a device, account, domain or machine.

## What a mnemonic anchor may do

It may help a person:

- locate an evidence package;
- prove access to one recovery path;
- trigger protected review;
- identify event handles or witness records;
- begin communication with relay agents.

## What a mnemonic anchor must not do

It must not by itself:

- transfer assets;
- release archives;
- change payee;
- activate succession;
- override inheritance law;
- prove total authority over the person;
- become the person.

## Storage guidance

A mnemonic may be:

- memorized;
- stored on paper;
- engraved on durable material;
- split among trusted parties using an appropriate secret-sharing method;
- sealed inside a legal or material anchor package.

Do not publish a mnemonic phrase in a `/.well-known/person-continuity` profile.

Do not email a mnemonic phrase casually.

Do not make memory the only root for high-risk continuity.

## Human failure

Memory can fail through trauma, coercion, dementia, illness, fear, aging, confusion or death.

Loss of a mnemonic must not erase personhood.

Disclosure of a mnemonic must not transfer personhood.

## Core rule

Mnemonic continuity gives mobility, but not immortality.
