# Personal Continuity Protocol
## Code of Personal Sovereignty - Public Seed Specification v1.0

**Status:** Public Seed Package v1.0 / implementation layer  
**Author:** Navi Musaget  
**Date:** 19 June 2026  
**Relation to Core Paper:** aligned with Personal Continuity Protocol Core Paper v1.0 Canonical  
**Purpose:** practical templates, schemas and instructions; not a final legal standard.

---

## 1. Positioning

This is not a system of identification.

It is a system of insurance against rupture.

Modern institutions often treat a person as if they exist only through a current interface: account, passport, bank channel, platform profile, KYC record, key, email, legal name, domain, repository, publisher profile or payment recipient.

The Personal Continuity Protocol asserts a different principle:

> A person must not disappear when the door changes.

The protocol protects continuity of rights, work, authorship, reputation, archives, contractual claims and limited delegated authority across changes of name, account, platform, jurisdiction, payment channel, key, silence, incapacity and death.

PCP does not replace law. It gives law, platforms and counterparties a better evidence trail when the interface breaks.

---

## 2. Core distinction

The protocol separates:

- **Personhood** - non-transferable human standing.
- **Identity** - a contextual relation between a subject and a name, identifier, account, document, role or credential.
- **Credential** - a document, account, token, passport, DID, certificate, key or record.
- **Interface / Door** - a platform, bank, registry, app, publisher, exchange, government portal, domain or API.
- **Continuity** - the evidentiary chain connecting past and present rights, obligations, work, reputation, authorship and lawful authority.

A credential may expire.  
An interface may close.  
A document may be revoked.  
Continuity must remain contestable, recoverable and portable.

---

## 3. Data minimization

The Continuity Kernel must not become a biography.

A seed package should contain or reference only what is needed to prove continuity for a specific purpose:

- hashes;
- timestamps;
- event handles;
- public anchors;
- witness records;
- material anchor references;
- contact routes for lawful notice;
- limited policy pointers;
- revocation or dispute markers.

It should not contain private keys, seed phrases, passports, medical files, family secrets, raw KYC materials, full correspondence, behavioral telemetry or full archives by default.

Continuity is not confession.

---

## 4. State-as-Node, not State-as-Owner

The protocol does not deny the role of states. States are powerful public anchor nodes for birth, death, marriage, inheritance, citizenship, courts, taxation and enforcement.

But state recognition of a fact does not make the state owner of the whole person.

> Root facts may be state-issued. Continuity must be multi-anchored.

The same principle applies to banks, platforms, notaries, stewards, foundations and technical operators. Each can be a node. None should be the whole person.

---

## 5. Cold Continuity

A free person must be able to go offline without becoming legally dead.

Absence of online activity is not death.  
Inactivity is not abandonment.  
Silence is not consent.  
Cold continuity is continuity without performance.

A subject may enter or remain in a dormant/cold state for months or years. This must not by itself trigger inheritance, archive disclosure, payee transfer, account seizure or delegated control.

Silence may increase caution. It must not transfer rights.

---

## 6. Clause 32

Clause 32 is the first adoption path.

It is a contractual clause stating that when a person changes name, key, account, jurisdiction, payment channel, platform profile, representative or successor claim, the counterparty must not treat that change as an automatic break in rights if a valid continuity package or continuity notice is provided.

Clause 32 does not force blind acceptance. It creates a reasonable procedure:

1. continuity notice received;
2. risky action paused;
3. evidence checked;
4. disputed funds or control held in escrow, suspense or protected pause;
5. competing claims allowed to respond;
6. legal/KYC/AML requirements preserved;
7. decision recorded.

Recognition is voluntary until notice makes ignorance expensive.

---

## 7. LARS - Legal Anti-Race System

After death, incapacity, account compromise, payee change, key change, disputed successor claim or contested platform takeover, the default action should not be transfer.

The default action is preserve, pause and verify.

LARS prevents the first actor who captures a key, account, heir claim or platform interface from winning before law catches up.

Pause may be easy. Transfer must be difficult.

---

## 8. Emergency Maintenance Mandate

For open-source and infrastructure projects, PCP allows a narrow emergency mandate:

- security patches may be applied;
- critical vulnerabilities may be mitigated;
- package integrity may be preserved;
- emergency releases may be published only within scope;
- actions must be logged and time-limited;
- no ownership transfer;
- no license change;
- no archive disclosure;
- no payee change;
- no author replacement;
- no succession claim.

Maintenance is not ownership. Security is not succession.

---

## 9. Steward, witness, validator and relay separation

No single steward may hold the whole person.

Roles must be separated:

- **Technical Operator** - hosts mirrors, APIs, schemas or uptime.
- **Legal Agent** - interacts with courts, notaries, platforms or contracts.
- **Continuity Steward** - helps maintain the package and reminders.
- **Blind Escrow Custodian** - stores encrypted material without reading it.
- **Witness Node** - records hashes, timestamps, receipts, dispute markers or event existence.
- **Validator** - evaluates whether a package is formally sufficient for a defined process.
- **Relay Agent** - helps a subject locate records, notify actors and initiate review.

A witness records. A validator evaluates. A relay transmits and assists.

A relay agent does not acquire authority by relaying.

The steward must be split before it becomes sovereign.

---

## 10. Event records and event handles

The Witness Commons should record events, not global biographies.

A continuity event may record:

- package creation;
- package update;
- material anchor deposit;
- Clause 32 notice;
- key transition notice;
- payee change notice;
- LARS pause;
- emergency maintenance activation;
- incapacity review;
- succession review;
- revocation;
- dispute marker;
- public root checkpoint.

An **Event Handle** is a non-biographical handle that helps locate an event record.

It must not become a global person number.

Event handles index continuity events, not persons.

---

## 11. Material and mnemonic anchors

A digital continuity proof needs a material witness.

A material anchor may be a printed fingerprint sheet, sealed paper package, notarial deposit, lawyer-held copy, archive-held copy, signed witness copy, metal/durable hash plate or offline storage medium with a human-readable cover sheet.

A mnemonic anchor may help a person initiate recovery without a device, domain or login.

But:

- loss of a mnemonic must not erase personhood;
- disclosure of a mnemonic must not transfer personhood;
- a mnemonic opens verification, not immediate control;
- no seed phrase should be published in web discovery;
- high-risk recovery needs material, institutional or class-separated support.

The recommended model is tri-anchor:

1. material anchor;
2. mnemonic recovery path;
3. institutional/witness anchor.

---

## 12. Bare-Body Recovery

Bare-Body Recovery is the ultimate human test.

A person may lose passport, phone, laptop, bank card, paper package, login, device, money and local institutional help. That person should not become a legal zero merely because every interface was lost.

Bare-Body Recovery means the right to initiate recovery.

It does not mean the right to be automatically believed.

It may help a person:

- contact a relay agent;
- locate public event handles or witness records;
- request a protected pause;
- identify material anchors stored elsewhere;
- begin class-separated verification;
- rebuild a court-readable evidence bundle.

Recovery may begin with memory. Transfer must not.

---

## 13. Adversarial Anchor Architecture

Human weakness cannot be removed. It must be distributed.

A high-risk continuity action should not rely only on confirmations from actors in the same power class.

Potential anchor classes include:

- subject / mnemonic / declaration;
- material anchor;
- family or personal witnesses;
- notary, lawyer or fiduciary;
- state root-fact node;
- platform, publisher, counterparty or foundation;
- independent archive, library, university or repository;
- professional association or community;
- technical public anchor or ledger;
- insurance, mutual defense or legal-defense layer;
- witness-commons node outside the local power chain.

Class-separated quorum means that three signatures from the same power chain are weaker than three confirmations from genuinely different classes.

No class of power may confirm itself.

---

## 14. Blind Legacy and succession

Blind Legacy can preserve encrypted drafts, code, archives or instructions without letting a steward read them during life.

But decryption is not succession.

Technical access must be separated from legal entitlement, safety review and probate or inheritance procedure.

A blind legacy arrangement should include an escalation path for steward disappearance, refusal, incapacity or death:

1. notice;
2. delay;
3. protected pause;
4. class-separated review;
5. legal/probate bridge;
6. limited technical release only if legally authorized.

A key may open a box. It does not decide who owns what is inside.

---

## 15. Medical Bridge and Hot Envelope

Incapacity should activate support, not replace the person.

A Hot Envelope may contain instructions for incapacity, but activation should be scoped, time-limited and reviewable.

Emergency support may require a lighter threshold where formal institutions are unavailable. Permanent transfer requires legal or class-separated review.

Inability to sign is not absence of personhood.

---

## 16. Minimal web discovery

A profile, author site, project site, publisher page or repository may expose:

```text
/.well-known/person-continuity
```

The endpoint is a cache and discovery pointer, not the source of truth.

A domain can be lost, seized, transferred, censored or misconfigured. Therefore:

- web discovery should point to evidence, not contain the whole person;
- endpoints should be mirrored;
- hashes should be archived outside the domain;
- material anchors should exist for high-risk packages;
- witness-commons records should exist;
- loss of a web endpoint should trigger recovery, not erase continuity.

No hosting layer is sovereign.

---

## 17. Protocol economics

Funding may sustain the protocol. Funding must not govern the person.

Witness nodes, archives, notaries, lawyers, platforms, foundations or technical operators may need resources to preserve records and respond to notices. Possible funding modes include volunteer operation, grants, institutional budgets, service fees, notarial fees, archival deposits, legal-defense funds or mutual aid.

Payment for storage or review must not become authority over continuity.

A commercial steward must not become a private sovereign.
