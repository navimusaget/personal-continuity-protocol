# Emergency Maintenance Mandate

**Draft template - not legal advice. Adapt by qualified counsel and project governance.**

## Purpose

This mandate allows limited emergency technical maintenance when a maintainer, author, project owner or key holder is unavailable, incapacitated, deceased, unreachable, offline or subject to a continuity dispute.

The purpose is preservation and safety, not succession.

## Permitted actions

The emergency maintainer may:

- apply security patches;
- mitigate critical vulnerabilities;
- update dependency references when needed for security or integrity;
- publish security advisories;
- publish limited emergency releases within scope;
- preserve repository/package integrity;
- document all actions taken;
- activate or maintain a LARS protected pause for disputed ownership transitions.

## Prohibited actions

The emergency maintainer may not:

- transfer ownership;
- change license terms;
- sell or assign project rights;
- remove or rewrite author attribution;
- access private archives unless separately authorized;
- change payee information;
- alter succession instructions;
- claim personal continuity of the original maintainer;
- represent maintenance as succession.

## Activation threshold

Activation should require defined evidence, such as:

- critical vulnerability or operational necessity;
- documented unavailability of the principal maintainer;
- confirmation by at least [number] independent maintainers, foundation officers, security contacts or legal/fiduciary actors;
- notice to listed contacts where safe and lawful.

For urgent security actions, emergency preservation may occur quickly. Ownership transfer must not.

## Duration

This mandate expires on the earlier of:

- resolution of the continuity dispute;
- return or recovery of the principal maintainer;
- appointment of a legal successor;
- expiration date stated in the mandate;
- court/platform/foundation decision;
- revocation by an actor with valid authority.

## Logging

Every emergency action should be logged with:

- date and time;
- actor;
- reason;
- scope;
- files / releases affected;
- hashes or commit identifiers;
- public notice where appropriate.

## Principle

Maintenance is not ownership. Security is not succession.
