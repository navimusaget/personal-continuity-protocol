# Clause 32 - Continuity of Subject, Rights, and Payment Channel

**Draft template - not legal advice. Adapt by qualified counsel.**

## 32.1 Continuity Notice

If a party, author, contributor, maintainer, rightsholder, payee, executor, legal agent or authorized representative provides a written Continuity Notice referencing a Personal Continuity Package, the receiving party shall not treat a change of name, pseudonym, account, key, jurisdiction, payment channel, platform profile, steward, relay agent or authorized representative as automatic termination, abandonment, transfer or forfeiture of rights.

## 32.2 Review and pause

Upon receipt of a Continuity Notice, the receiving party shall take reasonable steps to preserve the affected rights, records, payment status and project integrity while reviewing the notice.

Where competing claims, suspected compromise, incapacity, death, payee conflict, key-race, account takeover or successor dispute exists, the receiving party may place disputed payments or high-risk changes into escrow, suspense, administrative hold or protected pause until sufficient evidence, agreement, legal authority or court order is provided.

## 32.3 Pause / change / transfer

The receiving party should distinguish:

- **Pause** - preserving status and preventing immediate harm;
- **Change** - modifying a live continuity route;
- **Transfer** - moving rights, ownership, payee control, archive control, project authority or succession.

Pause may be easier. Transfer must be harder.

## 32.4 No biography demand

The receiving party shall request only evidence reasonably necessary to evaluate continuity, authority, payee change, succession or legal compliance, unless broader disclosure is required by applicable law, KYC/AML, court order, tax obligation or other mandatory legal process.

Continuity evidence is not consent to disclose a full biography.

## 32.5 No immediate transfer by silence

Absence of response, dormant status, cold continuity or offline life shall not alone constitute death, abandonment, incapacity, waiver, assignment, consent to transfer or authority for archive disclosure.

## 32.6 Good-faith notice

A receiving party that ignores a properly delivered Continuity Notice may have its conduct considered in later evaluation of good faith, reasonable care, negligence or contractual compliance, subject to applicable law.

## 32.7 Legal limits

Nothing in this clause requires a party to violate mandatory law, sanctions, tax obligations, KYC/AML rules, court orders or lawful regulatory instructions.

Where mandatory law prevents payment or transfer, the receiving party should preserve records and, where lawful, maintain the claimant's continuity status rather than treating the claim as extinguished.

## 32.8 No automatic acceptance

A Continuity Notice does not require blind acceptance of the claimant's position.

It requires preservation, reasonable review and avoidance of irreversible error where the transition is high-risk or disputed.
